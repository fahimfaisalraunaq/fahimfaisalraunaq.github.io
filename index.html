Profile

About

Trained as an Industrial & Production Engineer at BUET, I move between data-driven safety analytics, operations/supply-chain thinking, and language technologies for Bangla. I enjoy building systems that are not only accurate on paper but also deployable in low-resource settings.

Research narrative

My work explores how human behavior, infrastructure, and digital traces (e.g., social media, incident logs) can be integrated into interpretable risk and decision frameworks. I combine ML, multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA), and simulation/optimization to answer questions like:

  • How do spatio-temporal patterns of crashes evolve under policy or design changes?
  • Which interventions give the best trade-off between safety, cost, and equity?
  • How can noisy Bangla text be turned into reliable, decision-ready signals?

Working principles

A few rules I try to obey:

  • Reproducibility first: code, data, and pipelines that others can actually rerun.
  • Decision-support, not just prediction: models must speak to planners, clinicians, or policymakers.
  • Low-resource empathy: solutions that respect data sparsity, noise, and local constraints.

Research tracks

Two recurring threads: (1) risk and safety in socio-technical systems, and (2) making under-resourced language data behave as useful input to those systems.

Track A ¬∑ Safety & Decision Analytics

Data-driven safety for transport and construction, with an emphasis on interpretable risk metrics and policy-relevant insights.

  • Spatio-temporal crash modeling and severity analysis.
  • Accident causation frameworks blending ML with causal reasoning.
  • Decision-support tools combining MCDA, DEMATEL, and optimization.

Track B ¬∑ Low-Resource NLP & Digital Health

Building robust Bangla text pipelines that can plug into decision workflows in health, mobility, and online-safety contexts.

  • Bangla sentiment and toxicity corpora, stance detection.

  • Digital-health adoption and behavior modeling using text + survey data.
