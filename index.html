About

I’m a prospective Ph.D. researcher finishing an M.Sc. in Industrial & Production Engineering at BUET. I work across IE/OR, machine learning, and safety science to design end-to-end decision systems for high-stakes operations—manufacturing, supply chains, and transportation. My emphasis is on traceability and auditability so that quantitative models can support accountable decisions in real organizations.

Current work follows two tracks: (1) low-resource NLP for Bangla user-generated content, benchmarking MT-based vs. native models; and (2) policy-driven safety analytics in transportation and construction, using spatio-temporal and causal methods to surface actionable countermeasures. I’m seeking a Ph.D. program with strong clinical/industrial partnerships, a reproducible research culture, and opportunities to deploy methods in resource-constrained contexts.

Selected Work

M.Sc. Thesis — in progress

Supervisor: Dr. Nafisa Mahbub

Machine Translation vs. Language-Specific Models: A Benchmarking Framework for Low-Resource Sentiment Analysis

  • Linked MT quality (BLEU/chrF/SacreBLEU/COMET) to downstream macro-F1/accuracy on a gold-standard Bangla dataset.
  • Stress-tested English pipelines under MT noise; audited failure modes on phenomenon-tagged slices.
  • Ran translation-free baselines; validated “comment-as-a-unit” with ablations and McNemar tests.

Undergraduate Thesis — May 2023

Supervisor: Dr. Nafisa Mahbub

An Unsupervised Machine Learning Approach to Unfold Online Toxicity Pattern: A Look at the Bangladeshi Community

  • Built an ~18k-comment Bangla toxicity corpus with context.
  • Used K-Prototypes on mixed features to reveal a “slow-burn” toxicity cluster and Dhaka-centered, male-skewed participation.
  • Contrasted human-coded signals with BanglaBERT, isolating social response vs. model bias.
