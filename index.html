About

I design data‑driven safety and decision systems for socio‑technical operations—manufacturing, supply chains, and human–technology work. My approach blends operations research and machine learning with human factors to model risk, optimize policies, and turn noisy operational data into dependable decisions. Recent work includes building a benchmarking pipeline for low‑resource sentiment analysis, mentoring global teams, and translating product requirements across industry and academia. I’m preparing for PhD research on safety analytics, human‑in‑the‑loop optimization, and operations at scale.

M.Sc. Thesis — in progress

Supervisor: Dr. Nafisa Mahbub

Machine Translation vs. Language‑Specific Models: A Benchmarking Framework for Low‑Resource Sentiment Analysis

  • Linked MT quality (BLEU/chrF/SAM/COMET) to downstream macro‑F1/accuracy on a gold‑standard Bangla dataset.
  • Stress‑tested English pipelines under MT noise using protected stopword lists and simple negation marking; audited failure modes on phenomenon‑tagged slices (negation, intensifiers, idioms, honorifics).
  • Ran translation‑free baselines (BanglaBERT, Bengali VADER); validated “comment‑as‑a‑unit” with sentence‑level ablations and McNemar tests.

Undergraduate Thesis — May 2023

Supervisor: Dr. Nafisa Mahbub

An Unsupervised Machine Learning Approach to Unfold Online Toxicity Pattern: A Look at the Bangladeshi Community

  • Built an ~18k‑comment Bangla toxicity corpus with context (reactions, timestamps, gender, location).
  • Used K‑Prototypes on mixed features to reveal a “slow‑burn” toxicity cluster and Dhaka‑centered, male‑skewed participation.
  • Contrasted human‑coded signals with BanglaBERT, isolating social response vs. model bias.
